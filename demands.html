Hospital VS TBA Demands

"When you go to the hospital, they ask for so many things and even if you cannot afford them, you have to buy," said Ajumma Idris.

According to her, "the painful thing is that those government nurses don't always return what you didn't use."

The Nasarawa mother of two who had her second baby at a TBA's said: "I only had to buy Dettol and spirit. I used the clothes I wore when I had the senior child, and I took palm kernel oil and shea butter along with me. In the hospital I would have been ashamed of the smell and would have gone to buy baby oil or some other fancy item and new cloths.

"Many of us are forced to go out of our means in a quiet competition with other mothers who come to hospitals with nice things," Idris added.

Crowning this, Idris said, is the fact that TBA deliveries are at no cost beyond a few yards of fabrics or GL jewelry to say 'thank you', unlike the bills which have to be paid in the hospitals.
