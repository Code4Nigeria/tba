Traditional Birth Attendants

Not all that glitters is gold

Although the women tell glowing tales of their TBA encounters, and others justify their visits to them, there are some others who tell a different story.

Madam Jane, a mother of three in Maraba, a suburb near the FCT, recalled her ordeal in the hands of the TBA and vowed never to visit any again.

"My first labour started very late in the night, and because my husband does not have a car, and considering the high charges in the private clinic in the neighbourhood, my husband took me to the TBA, I nearly lost my life following the complications that arose, and instead of telling us the truth, the old birth attendant kept us in her house for three days and I was suffering seriously. I nearly died," Jane said.

Another woman, Ada said she almost died patronizing a TBA thinking hospitals were expensive. "We had just moved into Abuja from Imo State and considering the high cost of things in Abuja, I went to a popular TBA near my house thinking I was cutting cost, not knowing that I was digging my own grave."

"My husband is a policeman and we didn't have enough money for me to travel to the village to enable my mother attend to me, so I registered with this woman near my house. I am lucky to be alive today. The hygienic situation in her home is not good, and she was patching me up with any cloth she could lay her hands on when I started bleeding," Ada added.
